Sangsuk Yoon

Welcome!

I am an Associate Professor of Marketing at University of Dayton. My research examines the cognitive biases underlying human decision making, with a focus on how these biases shape consumer behavior in real-world contexts.

I study how findings from laboratory studies in judgment and decision making apply to various consumer decisions, including consumer preferential choices, financial decision making, and risk communications. My work bridges cognitive psychology and consumer behavior to understand not just what decisions people make, but why they make them.

I am also interested in using a multi-methodological approach, including behavioral, computational modeling, eye-tracking, and fMRI, to obtain converging evidence about the underlying mechanisms of cognitive biases and decision making processes.
